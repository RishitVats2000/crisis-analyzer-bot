The COVID-19 Economic Crisis

OVERVIEW
The COVID-19 pandemic caused the deepest global recession since World War 2. Beginning in early 2020, it triggered unprecedented government interventions, market crashes, and economic disruptions worldwide. Unlike the 2008 crisis which was financial, this was a real-economy crisis caused by health emergency lockdowns.

CAUSES
The crisis was triggered by the COVID-19 pandemic causing nationwide lockdowns. Supply chains broke down globally. Consumer spending collapsed as people stayed home. Tourism, aviation, and hospitality sectors shut down completely. Oil demand crashed leading to negative oil prices in April 2020.

EARLY WARNING SIGNS
Several warning signs appeared in early 2020. Wuhan lockdown in January 2020 was the first major signal. Italian and European hospitals overwhelmed in February. Global supply chain disruptions started in early February. VIX volatility index spiked to record levels in March 2020. Yield curve indicated recession expectations.

KEY EVENTS
March 9, 2020: Global stock markets crashed, called Black Monday. S&P 500 fell 7.6% triggering circuit breaker.
March 11, 2020: WHO declared COVID-19 a pandemic.
March 12, 2020: Stock markets had worst day since 1987 crash.
March 15, 2020: Federal Reserve slashed interest rates to zero.
March 27, 2020: US passed CARES Act, $2.2 trillion stimulus.
April 20, 2020: US oil prices went negative for first time in history.
May 2020: US unemployment reached 14.7%, highest since Great Depression.

IMPACT
Global GDP contracted by 3.4% in 2020, worst since 1945. Indian GDP contracted by 7.3% in fiscal year 2020-21. US unemployment jumped from 3.5% to 14.7%. Global stock markets fell 30% in March 2020. Over 100 million people pushed into extreme poverty globally. Small businesses devastated worldwide. Aviation industry lost over $370 billion in revenue.

SECTORS MOST AFFECTED
Aviation and travel sector collapsed with airlines losing 60% revenue. Hospitality and tourism shut down globally. Oil and gas industry crashed with negative prices. Retail apart from essentials hit hard. Entertainment industry froze. Energy sector saw massive losses.

SECTORS THAT THRIVED
Technology and software boomed with work from home. E-commerce saw unprecedented growth. Online streaming services gained millions of subscribers. Healthcare and pharmaceuticals surged. Cybersecurity demand exploded. Online education and edtech expanded rapidly.

RECOVERY MEASURES
Central banks cut rates to near zero globally. Quantitative easing programs were massively expanded. Government stimulus reached unprecedented levels worldwide. US passed multiple stimulus packages totaling over $5 trillion. India announced Atmanirbhar Bharat package of Rs 20 lakh crore. Vaccine development accelerated unprecedently.

LESSONS LEARNED
The crisis taught important lessons. Real economy shocks differ from financial shocks. Healthcare system resilience matters for economy. Digital infrastructure is critical. Government intervention can prevent total collapse. Stock markets and real economy diverged sharply. Speed of recovery depends on virus control.

COMPARISON WITH 2008
COVID was a real economy crisis, 2008 was financial. COVID recovery was much faster, took months not years. Government response was 2-3x larger than 2008. Asset prices recovered much faster than employment. Both crises required massive central bank intervention. Both showed importance of fiscal stimulus.
